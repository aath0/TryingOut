These are some of the research questions that keep me busy:

How do we extract regularities from our environment?

In our everyday lives we are exposed to streams of sensory information such as images, speech or music. Very often, these streams do not occur in a random way but follow statistical rules and repetitive patterns. My goal is to understand how we detect sensory rules and construct models of our environment.

How is our perception influenced by attention or awareness?

Perception of our environment may occur explicitly, as for example when we try to learn a new song, or implicitly, when for example we recognise a song while being occupied with other tasks. I am interested in studying which brain functions enable sensory perception as a function of attention or awareness.

Which neural functions are preserved in coma?

Patients who are in a coma are often not aware of their surroundings. During my PhD I have been interested in studying how environmental regularities are processed by comatose patients, in the absence of consciousness.

And these are some of the techniques that I'm using to answer them:

Electrophysiological recordings (EEG / iEEG)

Behavioral models

Psychophysiological recordings

Machine learning techniques
